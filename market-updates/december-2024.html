Issue: December 2024  |  Cheviot Hills & Beverlywood, Los Angeles

This Month's Article

By Ben Lee T here’s been so much uncertainty since the election.

Are we headed toward a dictatorship? Will millions of people suddenly be deported?

Will we enter in to WWIII with Russia? If confirmed, will the DOJ be led by an alleged criminal and the head of Health and Human Services ban vaccines?

Or maybe Elon Musk will give us all free Teslas and round-trip tickets to Mars.

Perhaps America will transform into a mythical golden land like Oz and we’re all about to become Dorothy, twirling around in a cloud of extra cash thanks to tax breaks and cheaper eggs?

These are topics way too complex to tackle in a small hometown newsletter.

Truth is, none of us knows what’s around the bend and, at least for me, speculating breeds anxiety.

Tranquility is only attained in situations over which I have some control and ownership (which is probably why I’m rarely, if ever, truly calm).

I like design and construction because I can make a decision and see it put into action.

I like real estate deals when there are problems I can help solve.

Sometimes there are not simple, clean solutions but even the negotiation process can be soothing if everyone can come to the table willing to listen, adapt expectations and ultimately compromise.

This month of holidays will be particularly conducive to compromise since for the first time in twenty years, the first night of Hanukkah happens to coincide with Christmas Day!

How fun and festive will that be? It will be a day in which pretty much everyone has reason to celebrate and share some laughs and gifts with loved ones.

Maybe you and your neighbor have celebrated either one or the other holiday in the past but this year you’ll decide to combine the two and share the day?

Turn it into a multi-cultural block party and every household can contribute a different offering symbolic of the holiday.

An overflowing buffet of latkes, mincemeat pie, brisket, ham, menorahs and Santa Claus sounds like a pretty fun party to me.

Then, a week later, turn that last night of Hanukkah into the best New Year’s Eve bash ever and invite all your neighbors who are now friends.

The real estate market generally slows down some during the holidays (and elections) and this year is no exception.

Buyers and sellers are taking a wait-and-see approach to see how the market will respond to the presidential race and virtually no one wants to get their homes in shape to sell when family members are all coming in town to visit.

So, I’m taking advantage of this (slightly) lighter schedule, too.

I’m looking forward to spending more time with my family, especially my two boys who’ll be home from college.

The five of us under one roof is much rarer now so I’ll be happy to just hang out and be together.

I’ll also be happy, refreshed and excited to tackle all those real estate needs coming my way in the New Year.

May these be delightful and joyous times for you and yours over the next few weeks.

However, for some of us, that’s easier said than done.

If you’re feeling a little lonely or wistful for those holidays of years past, that’s understandable too.

I hope you seek out a friend or neighbor and catch a movie or grab some dinner.

In other words, the holidays are terrific but can also be pretty tough for some people.

In these uncertain times, we all need to find happiness where we can and I hope that our community can provide that for those who need it most.

If not in a multi-cultural holiday block party, then at least a chat and cup of coffee at the corner?

Be kind and safe, everyone. Here’s to a very happy and healthy holiday season and festive New Year.
